## WHAT CHANGED AND WHY

### The Measurement Error
1. **Original Method:** Text search for "Italy" AND "China" → 18.65% (false positives)
2. **Second Method:** Sample of 8 elite institutions → 10.8% (sampling bias)
3. **Correct Method:** Institutional country codes → 3.38% (accurate)

### The Prestige Bias
- Initially sampled only elite institutions (Politecnico Milano, Bologna, etc.)
- Elite institutions have marginally higher international collaboration
- But even elite average (~4-5%) is close to national average (3.38%)
- Regional universities surprisingly similar (~4%)

### Implications
- **Research:** Italy has NORMAL China engagement, not elevated
- **Trade:** Dependency remains REAL and concerning
- **Policy:** Focus should shift from research restrictions to supply chain diversification

## FINAL ASSESSMENT

### The Reality
Italy faces a **NORMAL** research relationship but **ELEVATED** commercial dependency with China. The initial concern about anomalous academic collaboration was based on measurement error and sampling bias.

### The Real Risk
**Supply chain vulnerability** particularly in semiconductors (45% dependency) represents the primary technology security concern, NOT research collaboration.

### The Response
Policy should focus on **supply chain diversification** and **strategic autonomy** in critical sectors, while maintaining **open but monitored** research collaboration within EU frameworks.
