## Conclusion

Italy faces real but manageable vulnerabilities to Chinese economic coercion. The actual risk is significantly lower than initial data suggested:

- **Defense sector:** Protected (Leonardo has no China exposure)
- **Critical infrastructure:** Partially vulnerable (mainly telecom)
- **Economic dependency:** Moderate (not existential)
- **Research networks:** Limited risk (3.38% collaboration rate)

The path forward requires targeted action on genuine vulnerabilities (telecom equipment, medical devices, data sovereignty) while avoiding panic over non-existent threats. Italy can achieve resilience through systematic risk reduction rather than dramatic decoupling.

**Bottom Line:** Italy is vulnerable but not captured. With focused effort on the ~5% of truly critical contracts, the nation can maintain both economic engagement and strategic autonomy.
