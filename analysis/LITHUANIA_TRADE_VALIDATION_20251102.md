## Executive Summary

Cross-referenced GDELT media event coverage of "economic measures" (December 2021 peak) against actual Lithuania-China bilateral trade data from Eurostat. **Trade data validates GDELT's capture of asymmetric economic measures targeting Lithuanian exports.**

**Key Finding:** Lithuanian exports to China decreased 65.3% in 2021 and continued declining through 2023, while Lithuanian imports from China increased 34.4% in 2021 and 26.1% in 2022. This confirms targeted restrictions on Lithuanian goods entering Chinese market, not comprehensive bilateral trade restrictions.

## Methodology

### Data Extraction

**Database:** F:/OSINT_WAREHOUSE/osint_master.db
**Table:** eurostat_comext
**Query Logic:**
```sql
SELECT
    CAST(SUBSTR(period, 1, 4) AS INTEGER) as year,
    flow,
    SUM(value_eur) as total_value
FROM eurostat_comext
WHERE reporter = 'LT' AND partner = 'CN'
  AND year BETWEEN 2020 AND 2023
GROUP BY year, flow
```

**Flow Codes:**
- `1` = Import (Lithuania importing from China)
- `2` = Export (Lithuania exporting to China)

### Data Limitations

**Eurostat COMEXT Structure:**
- Period field: YYYYWW format (year + week number)
- Weekly granularity, not daily (cannot pinpoint exact event dates)
- Trade value in EUR (currency fluctuations possible)
- Quantity in KG where applicable (not all products)

**Analysis Constraints:**
- Annual aggregation used (quarterly breakdown showed data quality issues)
- Cannot isolate specific months (December 2021 GDELT peak)
- Product-level analysis not yet performed
- No comparison to other EU countries
