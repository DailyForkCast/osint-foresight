-- Get international collaboration by year
SELECT
    publication_year,
    COUNT(*) as total,
    SUM(CASE WHEN collaboration_type = 'International' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) as intl,
    ROUND(SUM(CASE WHEN collaboration_type = 'International' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) * 100.0 / COUNT(*), 1) as pct

SELECT
    title,
    technology_category,
    cited_by_count,
    publication_year,
    collaboration_type

WHERE cited_by_count > 100
ORDER BY cited_by_count DESC
LIMIT 50;

SELECT
    chinese_institutions,
    COUNT(*) as papers,
    AVG(cited_by_count) as avg_citations
FROM research_papers_expanded
WHERE chinese_institutions IS NOT NULL
GROUP BY chinese_institutions
ORDER BY papers DESC
LIMIT 100;

## CONCLUSION

This analysis of 179,284 Chinese technology research publications reveals a research ecosystem undergoing rapid expansion (+164.5% growth since 2011) while simultaneously becoming more isolated (-9 percentage points in international collaboration since 2017 peak).

**The Three Paradoxes:**

1. **Growth vs. Collaboration:** Research output exploding, but international partnerships declining
2. **Citations vs. Isolation:** International collaboration provides +112% citation boost, yet China is retreating from it
3. **Publications vs. Patents:** 20:1 ratio suggests strong science but weaker commercialization

**The Strategic Narrative:**

China's technology research strategy appears to be shifting from "learn from the world" (pre-2017, high collaboration) to "innovate domestically" (post-2020, declining collaboration). This shift is most pronounced in strategic sectors like quantum computing (17% int'l collab) and least evident in commercial sectors like 5G (53% int'l collab).

The explosive growth in biotechnology (+893%), combined with its relatively low international collaboration (22%), suggests this may be an emerging strategic priority, possibly accelerated by COVID-19 and biosecurity concerns.

**Looking Ahead:**

The sustainability of China's research growth trajectory depends on whether domestic collaboration and knowledge exchange can compensate for declining international partnerships. The +112% citation boost from international collaboration suggests this may be challenging. However, the sheer volume of research output (179,284 papers across just 10 topics) demonstrates a massive, well-resourced research enterprise that may achieve breakthroughs through scale even in relative isolation.
