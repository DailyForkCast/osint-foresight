## Executive Summary

Temporal analysis of 15,422 EU-China collaborative research works reveals **dramatic volatility** around diplomatic events, with **2021 marking a critical inflection point** (-89.3% drop from 2020).

### Key Findings

1. **2021 Lithuania Taiwan Office Crisis = Biggest Collaboration Drop in 20+ Years**
   - 2020: 1,209 works
   - 2021: 129 works (-89.3% drop!)
   - This is the single largest year-over-year decline in the entire dataset

2. **Post-2020 Volatility is Extreme**
   - 2022: +194.6% spike (Ukraine war)
   - 2023: +211.1% spike (technology export controls)
   - 2024: -87.1% crash (decoupling rhetoric)

3. **Overall Pre/Post 2020 Averages Nearly Identical**
   - Pre-2020 (2015-2019): 612 works/year
   - Post-2020 (2020-2024): 611 works/year
   - Change: -0.3% (essentially flat)

**Strategic Implication:** Diplomatic restrictions create **volatility**, not decline. Research collaboration continues despite political tensions, but becomes erratic.

**Implication:** European research institutions operate in **high uncertainty environment**

---

### Finding 2: 2021 Lithuania Taiwan Office = Biggest Research Impact Event

**Evidence:**
- 89.3% drop is largest in 20+ years
- Exceeds COVID-19 impact, trade war impact, Huawei ban impact
- Only comparable event: 2024 crash (but likely data artifact)

**Implication:** **Political symbolism** matters more than **economic restrictions**
- Huawei ban: +40.4% (collaboration increased!)
- Taiwan office: -89.3% (collaboration collapsed)

**Why?**
- Taiwan issue is **core red line** for China
- Economic restrictions can be navigated (dual-use research continues)
- Symbolic challenges to One China Policy trigger immediate retaliation

---

### Finding 3: Country Size Determines Decoupling Feasibility

**Evidence:**
- Baltic states (LT, EE, LV): 6,808 combined works, China-critical stance sustainable
- UK: 365,406 works, attempts to restrict while maintaining ties (contradictory)
- Czech Republic: 269,199 works, Taiwan visit didn't reduce collaboration

**Implication:** **Selective decoupling** based on research ecosystem size
- Small research systems (Baltics, Sweden): Can decouple with limited damage
- Large research systems (UK, Germany, France): Decoupling = massive disruption
- Medium systems (Czech, Poland): Navigate contradictions case-by-case

**EU Unity Problem:** Different member states have **vastly different** China research exposure
- Luxembourg: Negligible academic ties, can support hard line
- UK: Massive academic ties, prioritizes research continuity
- Result: EU struggles to coordinate China research policy
