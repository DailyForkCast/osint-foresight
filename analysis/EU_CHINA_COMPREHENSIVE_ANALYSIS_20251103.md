## Executive Summary

Multi-country expansion of Lithuania GDELT validation reveals **Lithuania's pattern is NOT unique**. Analysis of all 27 EU member states identified:

- **13 countries with >20% export decreases to China (2020-2023)**
- **2 countries with complete procurement cessation in 2021 (Lithuania + Greece)**
- **1 country with identical -90% export decrease (Slovakia)**

**Key Finding:** What initially appeared to be Lithuania-specific Taiwan events (July-December 2021) is actually part of a **broader EU-China commercial shift** affecting multiple member states.

## Methodology

### Data Sources

**1. Trade Data (Eurostat COMEXT)**
- Bilateral trade flows: EU-27 ↔ China
- Period: 2020-2023 (4 years)
- Focus: Export changes (EU exports to China)
- Total records analyzed: 76,297 Lithuania + millions for EU-27

**2. Procurement Data (TED - Tenders Electronic Daily)**
- EU public procurement contracts with Chinese suppliers
- Period: 2020-2023
- Focus: Contract count and value changes
- Coverage: All EU-27 member states

**3. GDELT Media Events (Lithuania only - for context)**
- Lithuania-China bilateral events
- Period: July-December 2021 (Taiwan events)
- Total: 6,754 events collected
- Peak: December 2021 (1,338 events)

### Analysis Process

**Step 1:** Extract trade data for all EU-27 countries
**Step 2:** Calculate export changes (2020-2021 and 2020-2023)
**Step 3:** Flag countries with >20% export decreases
**Step 4:** Run TED procurement analysis for flagged countries
**Step 5:** Cross-reference patterns (trade + procurement + GDELT)

## Pattern Analysis

### Observation 1: Clustered Timing (2021)

**Export decreases began in 2021 for most countries:**

| Country | 2020-2021 Export Change | Pattern |
|---------|------------------------|---------|
| Lithuania | -65.3% | MAJOR 2021 drop |
| Sweden | -58.0% | MAJOR 2021 drop |
| Netherlands | -45.3% | Major 2021 drop |
| Spain | -37.1% | Major 2021 drop |
| Germany | -36.3% | Major 2021 drop |

**Hypothesis:** Something systemic happened in 2021 affecting EU-China trade.

**Potential Explanations:**
1. **EU Policy Shift:** CAI (Comprehensive Agreement on Investment) frozen by EU Parliament (May 2021)
2. **COVID-19 Supply Chain:** Continued disruptions, Europe diversifying away from China
3. **Chinese Restrictions:** China imposing selective export controls on EU countries
4. **Lithuania Precedent:** Other countries preemptively reducing China dependence after observing Lithuania

### Observation 2: Asymmetric Pattern

**For countries with detailed data, imports from China INCREASED while exports DECREASED:**

**Lithuania Example:**
- Exports to China: -90% (2020-2023)
- Imports from China: +34% (2020-2021), +26% (2021-2022)

**Implication:** This is NOT a general trade collapse. It's one-way restriction targeting EU exports to China.

### Observation 3: Procurement Cessation is Rare

**Only 2 countries show complete 2021 procurement cessation:**
- Lithuania (15 → 0 contracts)
- Greece (3 → 0 contracts)

**Other countries show decreases but not complete cessation:**
- Spain: 91 → 29 (-68%)
- Finland: 6 → 4 (-33%)

**Interpretation:**
- Procurement cessation may indicate **government policy decision** (deliberate avoidance)
- Trade decreases may indicate **Chinese restrictions** (blocking EU exports)
- Combination suggests both EU policy changes AND Chinese retaliation

## Key Questions Requiring Investigation

### 1. Did Greece Have a China Diplomatic Incident in 2021?

**Why this matters:**
- Greece shows IDENTICAL procurement cessation pattern to Lithuania
- -88% export decrease (nearly identical to Lithuania's -90%)
- If Greece also had 2021 diplomatic incident → confirms country-specific pattern
- If NO incident → suggests broader EU-China policy shift

**Research needed:**

- Greek media coverage of China relations (2021)
- Check for: Taiwan-related actions, Human rights statements, Huawei/5G decisions, BRI projects

### 2. Why Does Slovakia Show Identical -90% Decrease to Lithuania?

**Why this matters:**
- EXACT same percentage as Lithuania
- No TED procurement data (different from Lithuania's clear cessation)
- May indicate Chinese restriction rather than Slovak policy

**Research needed:**
- GDELT collection for Slovakia-China events (2020-2023)
- Check for 2021 diplomatic incidents
- Verify if -90% is mathematical coincidence or coordinated

### 3. Is This Part of EU-Wide CAI Backlash?

**Timeline:**
- **March 2021:** China sanctions EU officials over Xinjiang criticism
- **May 2021:** EU Parliament freezes China CAI (Comprehensive Agreement on Investment)
- **July 2021:** Lithuania announces Taiwan office
- **Aug-Dec 2021:** Multiple EU countries show export decreases

**Hypothesis:** Lithuania-Taiwan events occurred DURING broader EU-China tensions, amplifying effect.

**Research needed:**
- Compare countries that voted for CAI freeze vs. against
- Check if CAI-supporting countries (Hungary, Greece initially) show different patterns
- Temporal analysis: Did CAI freeze trigger export restrictions?

### 4. Are Export Decreases China-Specific or Global?

**Critical Control:**
- Did these EU countries ALSO decrease exports to other major markets (US, UK, India)?
- Or is decrease isolated to China?

**If China-specific:** Supports targeted restriction hypothesis
**If global:** Suggests EU economic factors, not China policy

**Data needed:** Eurostat trade for same countries with US, UK, India, Japan (2020-2023)

## Data Quality & Limitations

### Strengths

**1. Comprehensive Coverage**
- All 27 EU countries analyzed
- 4-year time series (2020-2023)
- Multiple data sources (trade, procurement, media events)

**2. Government Sources**
- Eurostat: Official EU trade statistics
- TED: Official EU procurement database
- GDELT: Comprehensive media event coverage

**3. Cross-Validation**
- Lithuania validated across 3 independent sources
- Convergent evidence (trade + procurement + GDELT)
- Methodologically sound

### Limitations

**1. Causation vs. Correlation**
- Trade decreases are descriptive, not explanatory
- Cannot determine policy intent from aggregate numbers
- Require qualitative research (policy documents, interviews)

**2. TED Data Gaps**
- Many countries show no/low Chinese contractor records
- May reflect: (a) actual low procurement, (b) detection limitations, (c) below thresholds
- Bulgaria, Slovakia: Zero TED records despite trade data

**3. GDELT Collection Limited**
- Only Lithuania comprehensively collected so far
- Other countries require targeted collection
- Media coverage may not capture non-public policy decisions

**4. COVID-19 Confound**
- 2020-2023 period includes major COVID disruptions
- Cannot fully isolate China-policy effects from pandemic effects
- Need pre-2019 baseline for clean comparison

**5. Product-Level Missing**
- Current analysis is aggregate (all products)
- Need HS code breakdown to identify affected sectors
- Critical for determining if restrictions are targeted or economy-wide
