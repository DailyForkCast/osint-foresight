## EU-Level Framework (9 Documents)

### Artificial Intelligence
**1. Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 - AI Act**
- **Published:** July 12, 2024
- **Type:** Binding regulation across all EU member states
- **Strategic Significance:** World's first comprehensive AI regulation
- **URL:** https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj
- **Impact:** Sets legal framework for AI development, deployment, oversight - member states must comply
- **Risk-based approach:** Bans unacceptable AI uses, strict requirements for high-risk AI systems

**2. Coordinated Plan on Artificial Intelligence 2021 Review**
- **Published:** April 21, 2021
- **Type:** Strategy (coordinating member state actions)
- **Strategic Significance:** Coordinates €20B+ combined EU and member state AI investments
- **URL:** https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/library/coordinated-plan-artificial-intelligence-2021-review

### Quantum Technologies
**3. European Quantum Communication Infrastructure (EuroQCI) Initiative**
- **Published:** June 1, 2019
- **Type:** Infrastructure initiative
- **Strategic Significance:** Pan-European quantum-secure communication network covering all 27 member states
- **URL:** https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/european-quantum-communication-infrastructure-euroqci

### Semiconductors
**4. Regulation (EU) 2023/1781 - European Chips Act**
- **Published:** September 21, 2023
- **Type:** Binding regulation with €43B investment framework
- **Strategic Significance:** Aims to double EU global semiconductor market share to 20% by 2030
- **URL:** https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2023/1781/oj
- **Key Provisions:** State aid approval for fabs, coordinated supply chain monitoring, research funding

### Cybersecurity
**5. Directive (EU) 2022/2555 - NIS2 Directive**
- **Published:** December 27, 2022
- **Type:** Directive (requires transposition into national law by Oct 2024)
- **Strategic Significance:** Harmonized cybersecurity requirements across 18 critical sectors
- **URL:** https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2022/2555/oj

**6. Regulation (EU) 2019/881 - Cybersecurity Act**
- **Published:** June 7, 2019
- **Type:** Binding regulation
- **Strategic Significance:** Creates EU-wide cybersecurity certification framework, strengthens ENISA
- **URL:** https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2019/881/oj

### 5G/Telecommunications

## Key Policy Patterns and Insights

### 1. EU vs National Sovereignty Tensions
- **EU Level:** Binding regulations (AI Act, Chips Act, NIS2) set common framework
- **National Level:** Countries maintain sovereign AI/quantum/semiconductor strategies with own funding
- **Pattern:** EU provides regulatory harmonization; nations compete on R&D investment and industrial policy

### 2. Investment Levels by Technology Domain
- **AI:** €30B+ combined (EU + major member states)
- **Semiconductors:** €43B (EU Chips Act) + national co-investments
- **Quantum:** €4B+ combined (FR €1.8B, DE €650M, UK £1B, NL)
- **Digital Transformation:** €200B+ (IT €40B, ES €70B, FR part of France 2030)

### 3. Regulatory Approaches to AI
- **EU:** Comprehensive risk-based regulation (AI Act) - binding across member states
- **UK:** Principles-based, sector-specific approach - divergence from EU post-Brexit
- **National Strategies:** Focus on R&D investment, talent, ethical frameworks (complementary to EU regulation)

### 4. Cybersecurity Maturity Variation
- **Advanced:** France (ANSSI), UK (NCSC), Germany (BSI) - strong operational authorities
- **Developing:** Italy (ACN created 2021), Spain, Poland - newer institutional frameworks
- **Coordination Challenge:** NIS2 Directive aims to harmonize but implementation varies

### 5. Quantum Technology Leadership Race
- **Research Excellence:** UK (quantum hubs since 2014), NL (QuTech), FR, DE
- **EU Coordination:** EuroQCI initiative creates pan-European quantum communication backbone
- **Industrial Commercialization:** Still early stage, research-to-market gap remains

### 6. Semiconductor Sovereignty Push
- **EU Chips Act:** €43B to double market share to 20% by 2030
- **National Strategies:** DE (Intel Magdeburg), FR (STMicroelectronics expansion), UK (design focus)
- **Reality Check:** Competing with US CHIPS Act ($52B), China investments ($150B+), Taiwan/Korea dominance
